Heavy Rain Safety Advice

Author: ClimaCast, Updated: 23/05/2025

Heavy rain can lead to flooding, travel disruption, and danger on the roads. Warnings are issued when large amounts of rain are expected over a short or extended period.

ClimaCast issues heavy rain alerts based on forecasts from the Met Office and other trusted sources. Our warnings are designed to help you stay safe and prepared.

Before the Rain Starts

  • Check local forecasts and ClimaCast's Weather Alerts page for updates.
  • Move valuables and electronics off the floor in flood-prone areas.
  • Ensure gutters, drains, and grates are clear of leaves and debris.
  • Charge your phone in case of power outages.
  • Avoid parking your car in low-lying areas or under trees.

During Heavy Rain

  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters — just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
  • Stay indoors unless travel is absolutely necessary.
  • Keep children and pets away from swollen rivers, drains, and ditches.
  • If flooding occurs, turn off gas and electricity at the mains if it is safe to do so.
  • Watch for updates from emergency services and ClimaCast.

After the Rain

  • Do not return to flooded areas until it is declared safe.
  • Avoid contact with floodwater — it may contain sewage or chemicals.
  • Photograph any damage for insurance purposes before beginning cleanup.
  • Be cautious of weakened roads or structures.

Stay weather-aware and take action early — it could save lives and property.
