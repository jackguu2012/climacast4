Thunderstorm Advice

Author: ClimaCast, Updated: 19/05/2025

Risk of Flooding

Thunderstorms bring widespread precipitation, disruption, and potentially flooding. It is vital to keep your property and loved ones safe.

Our rain/thunderstorm warnings will tell you if your area is at risk of flooding. If this affects you, follow these steps:

  • Read the full Alert. It will give you vital safety information.
  • Prepare a flood plan. Our flooding article gives you a pre-built one.
  • Follow local authorities’ instructions such as sandbag collection, road closures, and emergency updates.

ClimaCast Alerts are designed to give you local information, but they do not replace official government or council instructions.

Lightning Safety Tips

Thunderstorms often include lightning, which can be extremely dangerous. Here are some ways to stay safe:

  • Stay indoors and avoid using plugged-in electronics.
  • Keep away from windows and metal objects.
  • Do not shelter under trees or near tall objects outdoors.
  • If you are outside with no shelter, crouch low with minimal contact to the ground.

Travel and Driving Tips

If you must travel during a storm, use extreme caution:

  • Turn on headlights and reduce speed.
  • Keep a safe distance between you and the vehicle in front.
  • Never drive through floodwaters. Even shallow water can stall your car or sweep it away.
