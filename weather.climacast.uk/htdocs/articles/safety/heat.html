Extreme Heat Advice

Author: ClimaCast, Updated: 18/05/2025

Extreme heat warnings are issued when temperatures rise well above normal for an extended period. These warnings are serious — they help protect lives and should be followed carefully.

ClimaCast typically issues these alerts in coordination with government health and weather services. The severity and visibility of our alerts may increase depending on public risk.

What to Do During a Heat Warning

If you receive a ClimaCast extreme heat warning, take the following steps:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking water regularly — avoid caffeine or alcohol.
  • Check in on older individuals and those living alone.
  • Close curtains or blinds that face direct sunlight.
  • Never leave children, pets, or anyone else in a locked car.
  • If outside, use sunscreen of at least SPF 30 and wear a hat.
  • Avoid going outside during peak UV hours (11 a.m. to 3 p.m.).
  • Walk in the shade and avoid heavy physical activities outdoors.
  • Take a bottle of water if you're going out for any length of time.
  • Follow ClimaCast on our Channel and view current Weather Alerts.

Always follow the safety advice in your specific warning — it’s tailored to your local risk and conditions.

If someone collapses due to heat, call 999 immediately and move them to a cool, shaded place while you wait for help.

Protect Your Pets

If the pavement is too hot for your hand, it’s too hot for your pet’s paws.

Use frozen water bottles wrapped in towels to help keep pets cool indoors. Make sure they have access to clean, fresh water at all times, and never leave them in a hot vehicle.

Cool Your Home Effectively

  • Close windows and blinds during the day to block out heat.
  • Open windows at night when temperatures drop to allow cool air in.
  • Use fans, or place a bowl of ice in front of one to chill the air.
