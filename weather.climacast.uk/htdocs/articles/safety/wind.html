Strong Wind Safety Advice

Author: ClimaCast, Updated: 23/05/2025

Strong winds can cause widespread disruption and danger. Follow this guide to stay safe when high winds are forecasted or currently happening.

ClimaCast will issue warnings if wind speeds could pose a risk to life or cause damage. These warnings will be based on data from official meteorological services.

Before the Wind Hits

  • Secure loose items outdoors like bins, garden furniture, and decorations.
  • Close and fasten all doors and windows tightly.
  • Park vehicles away from trees or anything that could fall.
  • Postpone unnecessary travel, especially in high-sided vehicles.

During Strong Winds

  • Stay indoors and keep away from windows and glass doors.
  • If you're outside, watch for falling branches, tiles, or debris.
  • Avoid walking near walls, scaffolding, or trees that may fall.
  • Do not touch downed power lines or cables — report them immediately.
  • Only travel if it's absolutely necessary and check public transport updates.

After the Wind

  • Check your property for damage safely.
  • Report fallen trees or debris blocking roads or paths.
  • Watch out for continued risks from unstable structures.
  • Follow updates from local authorities and ClimaCast for any continued alerts.

Strong winds can be dangerous even if they seem short-lived. Stay safe and keep informed through ClimaCast.
